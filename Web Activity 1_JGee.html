Cinnamon Spice

One of Earths best spices - An article and recipe by Josh Gee

    Cinnamon has been around for thousands of years. Cinnamon is known to have been around since at least 2000 BC and was once revered as a rare delicacy. Even being presented to rulers as a gift or a sign of wealth.

    Today you can find cinnamon in any grocery store or super market. It is one of the most commonly used spices in the world. In America cinnamon is used in many different types of deserts. Theres the classic cinnamon roll, where cinnamon is the main flavor, all the way down to various fruit flavored pies in which cinnamon is used as a flavoring agent.


Below will be a good old fashioned recipe for cinnamon rolls, because Anyone who gives you a cinnamon roll fresh out of the oven is a friend for life.   - Lemony Snicket

Grandma Delsie's Cinnamon Rolls

Ingredients:

3/4 cup milk
1/2 cup shortening
1 tsp salt
2 pkg active dry yeast 1/2 cup warm water (temperature < 120, but > 115)
4 1/2 cups flour
2 eggs
1 cup suagr
1/2 cup melted butter
1tbsp Cinnamon
1 cup confectioners' sugar
2 tbsp milk
1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Scald milk in a saucepan; add shortening, sugar, and salt. Cool to lukewarm.
Step 2: Sprinkle yeast on warm water; stir to dissolve.
Step 3: Add 1 1/2 cups of the measured flour to the milk mixture; beat well.
Step 4: Beat in eggs and yeast.
Step 5: Gradually stir in enough remaining flour, a little at a time, to make a soft dough.
Step 6: Turn onto lightly floured board cover and let rest for 5 minutes.
Step 7: Knead, incorporating a little more flour as necessary, until smooth, satiny, and no longer sticky, for about 8 to 10 minutes.
Step 8:Place in a lightly greased bowl, invert to grease top, cover, and let rise until doubled, for 1 to 1 1/2 hours. Punch down and turn onto a board.
Step 9: Divide in half. Lightly grease counter or board. Roll each half into a 14 x 6-inch rectangle.
Step 10: For the filling, combine 1 cup of sugar, 1/2 cup of melted butter, and 1 Tbsp ground cinnamon.
Step 11: Spread half of the mixture on each rectangle.
Step 12: Roll up the rectangle lengthwise. Seal the edges and cut dough into 1-inch slices. Put cut side down in 2 well-greased square baking pans. Cover and let rise until doubled. Brush butter on the tops very lightly.
Step 13: Bake 25-30 minutes in a preheated oven at 350 degrees.
Step 14: For the icing, mix 1 cup confectioners' sugar with milk or cream to enable spreading consistency. Add 1/2 tsp vanilla and a dash of salt.
Step 15: Spread icing over warm rolls.
